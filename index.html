Galileo Galilei

Galileo Galilei (1564–1642) was an Italian astronomer, physicist, and engineer, sometimes called the "father of modern science." He made pioneering observations that laid the foundation for modern physics and astronomy.

Major Discoveries & Inventions

Among his many contributions, Galileo improved the telescope and used it to observe celestial bodies. He discovered Jupiter’s moons, studied sunspots, and supported the heliocentric model of the solar system, challenging traditional beliefs.

Legacy

Despite facing opposition and even house arrest by the Inquisition, Galileo’s work profoundly shaped scientific thinking. Today, he remains a symbol of intellectual courage and scientific inquiry.
